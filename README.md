# 🛒 Sample Superstore EDA

## 📌 Project Overview

This project performs an Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) on the Sample Superstore dataset using Python. The objective is to uncover sales and profit trends, identify high-performing product categories, analyze customer segments, and generate actionable business insights through data visualization.

---

## 🛠️ Tools & Libraries Used

* Python
* Pandas
* NumPy
* Matplotlib
* Seaborn
* Jupyter Notebook

---

## 📂 Dataset

The dataset contains transactional sales records from a retail superstore, including:

* Order Date
* Sales
* Profit
* Category
* Sub-Category
* Customer Segment
* Region
* State
* Discount
* Quantity

---

## 🔍 Analysis Performed

### 1. Data Cleaning & Preparation

* Checked data types and missing values
* Converted date columns to datetime format
* Removed trailing spaces from column names and categorical values
* Prepared monthly aggregated datasets for trend analysis

### 2. Monthly Sales Analysis

* Analyzed sales trends over time
* Identified seasonal patterns and peak sales periods

### 3. Sales by Category

* Compared sales performance across product categories
* Identified the highest and lowest revenue-generating categories

### 4. Sales by Sub-Category

* Analyzed contribution of individual product sub-categories
* Created heatmaps to visualize monthly sales patterns

### 5. Monthly Profit Analysis

* Examined profit trends over time
* Compared profitability against sales performance

### 6. Profit by Category & Sub-Category

* Identified the most profitable product groups
* Highlighted categories with weaker profit performance

### 7. Sales & Profit by Customer Segment

* Compared Consumer, Corporate, and Home Office segments
* Evaluated each segment's contribution to overall business performance

### 8. Sales-to-Profit Analysis

* Analyzed the relationship between revenue and profitability
* Compared efficiency of different business segments

---

## 📊 Key Insights

* Sales exhibit clear monthly fluctuations and seasonal trends.
* A small number of categories contribute a significant share of total revenue.
* Certain sub-categories consistently outperform others in both sales and profit.
* High sales do not always result in high profits.
* Customer segments differ in their revenue and profit contributions.
* Profitability analysis provides deeper business insights than sales analysis alone.

---

## 💡 Business Recommendations

* Focus marketing efforts on high-performing categories and sub-categories.
* Monitor low-profit products and review pricing strategies.
* Optimize inventory planning around peak sales periods.
* Prioritize profitability alongside revenue when making business decisions.
* Develop targeted strategies for different customer segments.
